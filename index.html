Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is inbound marketing ?

    Inbound marketing is a marketing methodology that works on the principles of attracting customers by creating valuable content. It intends to provide the audience and readers with content that they want to read, as opposed to outbound marketing that creates interruptions.

  • What is a buyer persona ?

    A buyer persona is a fictional representation of your target audience. Every business or brand owner needs to be able to define their buyer persona in great detail. While creating a buyer persona, it’s vital to define the nitty-gritty such as: Demographics, likes and dislikes, psychographics, their online activity, what channels are they on, what kind of content do they consume. You must give a name to your buyer persona as this helps in creating more targeted content.

  • What does a digital marketer do ?

    A digital marketer assists in building a brand or a business’s presence online on various channels. A digital marketer wears many hats, often juggling multiple verticals such as strategy, campaigns, and creatives. Digital marketers often know all domains that lie under the purview of digital media, however, they choose to specialize in one or two domains at a time.

  • What do we mean by strategy ?

    When we say strategy, we mean a solid plan of action based on data, insights, a brand’s specific needs, and requirements. A digital strategy is a playbook that can be evolved. This playbook consists of a concrete roadmap that a brand can follow without overwhelming itself.

  • Would my business benefit from digital marketing ?

    In a hyper digitally connected world, no business wouldn’t benefit from digital marketing. Whether your business needs a digital presence or needs revamping, depends upon a 10 variety of factors. According to statista.com, digital advertising spends worldwide, amounted to 378.16 Billion USD, in 2020. By 2024, that number will increase to 645.8 Billion U.S Dollars. The best time to implement digital marketing was yesterday and the next best time is today.

  • What is marketing automation ?

    Marketing automation is the process of using software to automate marketing activities. Often, marketers use strategy and automation to seamlessly integrate their marketing efforts. Marketing automation is used primarily for repeatable tasks such as setting up email campaigns or social media scheduling.

  • What is data-driven marketing ?

    Data-driven marketing uses data to capture a customer’s insights, behaviour patterns, preferences to better understand an audience’s motivations. This allows marketers to craft compelling content and marketing strategies that target people who’re looking for solutions being sold by marketers. Without data, marketers would be shooting their shots without an aim.

  • What is performance marketing ?

    Performance marketing allows advertisers to pay platforms such as Facebook or Google to display their advertisements. Advertisers are charged when a specific action such as a click, sale, or sign-up takes place.

  • How do I differentiate my brand ?

    Brand differentiation is an evolving process. One of the most remarkable ways to differentiate your brand is by telling stories. A unique brand story captures people’s hearts. Every product or service has a stream of stories to tell. When a brand decides to engage in storytelling, it assures to keep things raw and authentic.

  • Do I need a professional copywriter ?

    Marketing is a game of perceptions. Words have a powerful way of influencing people’s thoughts and emotions which eventually drives them to take action. The same words, when used for a brand’s communication online, cause their audience to get inspired and take action. Such is the responsibility of a copywriter. A professional copywriter crafts communication for brands and moves people to take action.

  • What does Google Analytics do for me ? What should I measure ?

    Google Analytics is a powerful tool that allows you to track your audience’s device usage, social media activity, brand preferences, and the like. Based on this, you can build strategic digital marketing campaigns that are custom-built for your target audience and potential customers.

    Some of the most important metrics to measure are:
    1. Users
    2. Sessions
    3. Pages per session
    4. Devices
    5. Source/Medium and channels
    6. Pageviews, unique pageviews
    7. Average time on page
    8. Landing pages
    9. Exit pages
    10. Bounce Rate
